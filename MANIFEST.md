## The Solution: Atlas, the Web of Trust (WoT)

Montesquieu Model split power into Legislative, Executive and Judical branches. That model remains very reasonable and valid, but digital world has different rules of physics, power dynamics, which only some people understand.

In Atlas, we introduce "Separation of Concerns":
- **Semantics & Interpretation (Common Law)** - Defines the relationships between concepts, curated via transparent, open process agreement. Essentially, we democratically decide how to deal with information in a systematic, interoperable manner. Effectively we decide on detection and punishment of malicious actors and rules how our (Users) data MUST be interpreted where it matters.

- **Content (Judical)** - Envelopes secure ability of making independent "claims" about reality with a verified sources. Trust in verified sources allows us to choose what we believe in. There is no need for single "judge", because Envelopes reflect opinions and stance of our community on certain matter. Envelopes, mathematically interpreted with Common Law rules, effectively become a binary result, verifiable, deterministic result - if we should act and how we should act.

- **Storage (Body)** - In digital world, your "Digital Body" is currently rented from landlords (Big Tech), who can evict you at any moment. Your mouth might be forcibly closed. Your assets might be forcibly destroyed. As of time of writing this, there are still personal PC's and internet is not fully controlled, but if we don't act - soon you might be left with just a screen with AI-generated slop feed from the day you're born, until the day you die. Storage in Atlas provides the permanent, ownerless ground upon which your Envelopes rest. It is the transition from "Data as a Service" (where you are a guest) to "Data as a Body" (where you are the owner). By ensuring your data exists independently of any application, you gain the "right to exist" digitally, ensuring that your voice cannot be silenced by a "Delete" button held by any central power. Decoupling data from platforms and anchoring it to the individuals via cryptographic signatures, we move from a web of **gatekeepers** maximizing their profits, to a simple web of **trust**.

## The Cycle of Trust

The Web of Trust creates a self-reinforcing loop that protects the network's integrity:

1.  **Publishing:** Users sign Envelopes. These Envelopes contain not just content, but likes, trust-ratings, and verifications of other users. Deriving information from related Envelopes is primary source of objective, common truth.
2.  **Gossip:** Nodes Store and share the Envelopes. Trust propagates through the network.
3.  **Evaluation:** As users consume data, they issue more "Trust" or "Distrust" Envelopes.
4.  **Convergence:** Nodes prioritize the storage of high-trust data to remain relevant and useful to the community.
5.  **Pruning:** Malicious, unverified, or low-signal traffic is rejected at the Node level (**Resilience**), starving bad actors of the network's collective resources.
